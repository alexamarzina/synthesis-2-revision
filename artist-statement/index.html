Artist Statement

Even while attempting this project the first time, it seemed out of my league. Heck, even making a word cloud was difficult for me. BUT, I did it. As previously stated, this is a follow-up project of a word cloud that I made from the same dataset, and I wanted to further investigate the usage of song titles.


This graph is able to show the frequency of the word "love" in song titles, for songs written between 1930 and 2010 (or at least the ones that are recorded in the dataset). As you can see, love is a more recent topic in the musical world, which is probably contrary to what the world may think. Every song seems to be about love some how, whether it be about being in love, loving something (an object), or even breaking up after once being in love.


Making this through Plotly made it interactive; you can hover on the line at different years to see the year and the number of "love songs" produced in that same year. In order to do this, I had to take the data out of the csv and put it into individual lists. Then, through some Python fiddling, I was able to "count" how many instances of love occurred each year. Then, I just had to plug my variables into Plotly and it did a lot of the magic for me.


.

This may be cliche, but this project truly did help me grow as a media student and a person. Obviously, it enhanced my Python (and various other coding) skills, as I was able to produce a visualization that I couldn't previously. It also helped my troubleshooting/debugging skills, which needed some work and is most of the "skill" required to code anything successful. Because of these things, I've also learned how to be more patient with myself, something I've always struggled with.


This viz could be useful for anyone else who is a music fan and is interested in studying the theme of love in music over the years. Maybe a comparison could be made between songs and something like movies, which probably also have a large volume of love in them.


To conclude, while love certainly isn't a new theme or fad in music, it is definitely increasing in popularity as time goes on; according to the data, there is indeed an increase over time of the number of new songs that have the word "love" in the title.
